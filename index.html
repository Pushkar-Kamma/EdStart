Our Problem

Every year, 2.4 million immigrants arrive in the U.S., many with school-aged children. In King County, where immigrants make up 25% of the population, students face challenges like learning English, adapting to a new culture, and keeping up in school. Around 9.6% of public school students nationally are English Language Learners (ELLs), a trend reflected in King County, where resources often fall short.

Our Solution

Mentorship

We offer 3-month mentorships pairing students with trained mentors for guidance, goal-setting, and accountability.

Seminars

We bring in-school seminars and online workshops to explain classes, programs, and opportunities clearly.

Articles

Helpful guides and resources on our site—for families and educators—updated with practical tips students can use today.

Our Mission

Empower

Give families clear tools to navigate school with confidence—from enrollment to advanced classes.

Engage

Connect students, mentors, and educators to build supportive, culturally responsive communities.

Guide

Provide step-by-step pathways—ESL, AP/Honors, clubs, college readiness—so goals feel achievable.
